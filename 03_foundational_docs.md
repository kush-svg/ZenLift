> **How to use this:** Run through this checklist for every new client within 48 hours of receiving their first payment. The goal is to collect everything we need to do the work — so there are zero delays caused by missing client information during delivery.

---

**Business Name:** _______________
**Niche:** _______________
**Offering Purchased:** _______________
**Onboarding Date:** _______________
**Zenlift Lead on This Project:** _______________

---

### PHASE 0 — BEFORE WORK STARTS

**Payment & Paperwork**
- [ ] 50% advance payment received and confirmed
- [ ] Payment screenshot saved in client folder
- [ ] Proposal copy saved in client folder (PDF)
- [ ] Client added to Zenlift project tracker (Notion / Sheet)
- [ ] Estimated delivery date set and shared with client

**Communication Setup**
- [ ] Client added to dedicated WhatsApp group (Zenlift + Client)
- [ ] Team member assigned as client's primary point of contact
- [ ] Welcome message sent to client explaining what happens next

---

### PHASE 1 — INFORMATION COLLECTION (Brief Call or Form)

**Conduct a 30-minute brief call or send a brief form. Collect the following:**

**Business Basics**
- [ ] Full business name and tagline (if any)
- [ ] Business address (for Maps embed and local SEO)
- [ ] Operating hours (days + time)
- [ ] Phone number (primary — for WhatsApp CTA)
- [ ] Email address (for inquiry form notifications)
- [ ] WhatsApp number (for click-to-chat CTA — may differ from phone)

**Services / Offerings**
- [ ] Full list of services offered (in plain language, as client explains them)
- [ ] 2–3 most important or popular services (for hero section)
- [ ] Any pricing they want visible (many won't want pricing public — ask)

**Brand Assets**
- [ ] Logo file (ask for PNG with transparent background, or highest quality available)
- [ ] Brand colors (if any — ask for hex codes or describe colors)
- [ ] Photos of the clinic/center/business (minimum 5 good-quality photos)
- [ ] Photos of staff or doctor (if they consent)
- [ ] Existing website link (if any, for reference)
- [ ] Existing social profiles (Facebook, Instagram, etc.)

**For Chatbot / Lead Capture (Offering 2 only)**
- [ ] 10–15 most common questions from customers/patients (ask them to list these)
- [ ] Desired response for each question (they provide content; we format and write)
- [ ] What should happen when someone submits their contact info? (WhatsApp message? Call? Email?)

**Competitor Reference**
- [ ] Ask: "Are there 1–2 businesses in your area or in your field that you think have good online presence? Can you share the links?" (Useful for design reference — not copying)

**Outcome Goals**
- [ ] What does success look like for this client? (Write this down in their own words)
- [ ] What's the main thing they want this to do for their business?

---

### PHASE 2 — SETUP AND ACCESS

**Accounts and Access**
- [ ] Google Account access (for GBP management — ask for login or invite as manager)
- [ ] Google Business Profile verified? (If not, assist client through verification)
- [ ] Domain access (if they have one — get hosting panel or registrar login details)
- [ ] Instagram / Facebook access (if Growth Support is included)

**Tools Setup (Internal — team does this)**
- [ ] Client folder created in Zenlift Google Drive / Notion
- [ ] Client added to project management tool
- [ ] Figma / design file created with niche template as starting base
- [ ] Lead tracking Google Sheet created and shared with client (view-only link)

---

### PHASE 3 — DURING DELIVERY

**Milestone Checkpoints**
- [ ] Wireframe / design mockup shared with client for approval before build
- [ ] Client has reviewed and approved design direction (get explicit confirmation)
- [ ] First draft of copy shared with client for review
- [ ] Copy approved (with any edits applied)
- [ ] Website / page built in staging
- [ ] Client has reviewed staging version and signed off
- [ ] All forms and chatbot flows tested
- [ ] WhatsApp button tested on mobile
- [ ] All lead notifications tested (form submit → client gets WhatsApp alert)
- [ ] Google Business Profile updated and photos uploaded

**Quality Checks (Internal)**
- [ ] Mobile responsiveness tested on 3 different screen sizes
- [ ] Page speed acceptable (use Google PageSpeed Insights — aim for 70+ mobile)
- [ ] All links working
- [ ] Contact form submitting correctly
- [ ] Chatbot tested end-to-end with a real device

---

### PHASE 4 — DELIVERY AND HANDOVER

**Launch**
- [ ] Site / page moved to live domain
- [ ] DNS connected and propagated
- [ ] Final check on live site — all links, forms, and CTAs working

**Handover**
- [ ] Handover document prepared (see template below)
- [ ] Handover document shared with client via WhatsApp and email
- [ ] 30-minute walkthrough call conducted with client (screen share)
- [ ] Client walked through: how to see their leads, how to update basic info, who to contact for changes

**Payment**
- [ ] Remaining 50% payment requested
- [ ] Payment received and confirmed
- [ ] Receipt / acknowledgment sent

**Post-Delivery**
- [ ] Client added to Zenlift post-delivery follow-up schedule (check in at Day 14 and Day 30)
- [ ] Case study / testimonial request planned (ask at Day 30 if things are going well)
- [ ] Upsell opportunity noted in client record (what's the natural next offering for this client?)

# DOCUMENT 3 — DELIVERY SOP: OFFERING 1 (Digital Starter)

> **Purpose:** This SOP defines exactly how Offering 1 (Digital Starter) is delivered — step by step. Every team member should be able to follow this. Consistency is what makes delivery scalable. When Zenlift eventually builds a team or hires, this SOP is what trains them.

---

## DELIVERY SOP — OFFERING 1: DIGITAL STARTER

**Offering:** Website or Landing Page + GBP Optimization
**Typical Timeline:** 5–18 working days (depending on variant)
**Owner:** [Assigned team member]

---

### PRE-PRODUCTION (Days 1–2)

**Day 1 — Kickoff**
1. Confirm payment received. Create client folder in Drive.
2. Add client to Notion project board with status: "In Onboarding."
3. Send welcome WhatsApp message to client:
   > "Hi [Name], welcome to Zenlift! We've received your advance and we're starting on your project. Over the next 24 hours, I'll be sending you a short form to fill out — it takes about 10 minutes and gives us everything we need to get started. Let me know if you have any questions!"
4. Share onboarding brief form (Google Form) with client.
5. Set internal deadline for receiving brief: 48 hours.

**Day 2 — Brief Review**
1. Review completed brief for completeness. If anything missing, WhatsApp client immediately — don't wait.
2. Download all photos from client. Rename and organize in Drive.
3. Open niche-specific design template in Figma (Clinic / Coaching / Salon / Cafe / Real Estate).
4. Make a copy of the template and rename it for this client.
5. Update project status to: "Brief Complete — Design Starting."

---

### DESIGN PHASE (Days 3–5 for Landing Page / Days 3–8 for Website)

**Step 1 — Copy First**
Before designing, write the copy for every section. This order matters — design wraps around copy, not the other way around.
- Hero headline: Should state the outcome the business delivers for customers (not the features)
  - BAD: "Dr. Sharma Dental Clinic — Professional Dental Care"
  - GOOD: "Comfortable, Affordable Dental Treatment in [Area] — Book an Appointment Today"
- Services section: Use client's list from brief. Write 2–3 lines per service.
- About section: Brief, trust-focused. Mention years of experience, qualifications, and local presence.
- CTA sections: Every CTA should be specific. Not "Contact Us." Instead: "Request an Appointment on WhatsApp" or "Register for a Free Demo Class."
- Review/testimonial section: Use any real reviews from GBP or Facebook. If none, use placeholder text and note to client we'll add reviews once the platform is live.

**Step 2 — Wireframe**
- Create low-fidelity wireframe in Figma for the page layout.
- Share with team for a quick internal review (10 minutes). Any structural problems should be caught here, not after the full design is done.

**Step 3 — Full Design**
- Apply the niche-specific design to the wireframe.
- Use client's brand colors if provided. If not provided, use the niche default palette.
- Insert real photos from client where available. Use stock photos as placeholders only.
- Add WhatsApp button in header AND as a sticky CTA on mobile.
- Design for mobile first. Desktop second.

**Step 4 — Design Review**
- Internal review: Does this look credible and professional? Would you trust this business if you saw this page?
- Share Figma preview link with client.
- Message to client: "Hi [Name], here's a preview of your website design. Please take a look and let us know: (1) Does the content look accurate? (2) Does the overall look feel right for your business? Any changes before we build it live."
- Wait for approval. Do not begin build until client says "looks good" or "approved" in writing (WhatsApp counts).

---

### BUILD PHASE (Days 6–10 for website / Days 4–6 for landing page)

**Platform Selection (decide before build starts)**
- Framer: Use for modern, visual businesses (cafes, salons, fitness)
- Webflow: Use for professional service businesses (clinics, coaching, real estate) — slightly more structured
- WordPress (Elementor): Use if client explicitly needs to edit the site themselves, or if complex form integrations are required

**Build Steps**
1. Duplicate the appropriate platform template.
2. Transfer approved design into the build.
3. Set up contact form — connect to client email AND Zenlift monitoring email (so we know if forms break).
4. Set up WhatsApp click-to-chat button with pre-filled message:
   - e.g., "Hi, I found your clinic online and would like to book an appointment."
5. Embed Google Maps using the iframe code from the client's GBP listing.
6. Set up basic meta SEO:
   - Page title: [Business Name] | [Specialty] in [Area]
   - Meta description: 1–2 lines using the city and primary service keyword
   - Alt text on all images
7. Connect the domain (or set up on a subdomain if client doesn't have one yet).
8. Test all forms and CTAs on mobile.

**Quality Assurance Checklist Before Sharing with Client**
- [ ] Site loads in under 4 seconds on mobile (test with Chrome DevTools)
- [ ] WhatsApp button tested on a real phone
- [ ] Contact form submits and client receives email notification
- [ ] Google Maps loads and shows correct location
- [ ] All internal links work
- [ ] No placeholder text remaining (no "Lorem Ipsum")
- [ ] All photos are properly sized (no distortion)
- [ ] Mobile layout reviewed on small screen (iPhone SE size)

---

### REVIEW AND REVISION PHASE (Days 9–12)

1. Share staging link with client.
2. Message: "Hi [Name], your website is ready for review. Here's the link: [LINK]. Please take a look on both desktop and mobile. You have 1 round of revisions included. Please consolidate all your feedback into one message or list so we can apply everything at once. We'll turn around revisions within 2 working days."
3. Apply revisions.
4. Get final approval in writing.
5. Do not allow scope creep during this phase. If client asks for something outside the original brief, note it and quote it separately.

---

### GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE OPTIMIZATION (Parallel to build — Days 5–8)

1. Request manager access to client's GBP, or log in with client's Google account.
2. Complete / update the following:
   - Business name (exact and consistent)
   - Primary and secondary categories
   - Business description (250–300 words, keyword-rich, natural language)
   - Address and phone number
   - Operating hours (all days)
   - Upload minimum 10 photos: exterior, interior, staff, services
   - Enable messaging if not already active
   - Add at least 2 GBP posts (one about services, one about contact/booking)
3. If business is not verified on GBP, assist client through verification process (postcard or phone verification — takes 1–5 days).
4. Document what was done in the client's folder.

---

### LAUNCH AND HANDOVER (Days 11–15 for landing page / Days 15–18 for website)

**Launch Day**
1. Move from staging to live domain.
2. Wait 30–60 minutes for DNS propagation.
3. Do a final check on live URL — not staging URL.
4. Screenshot the live homepage and send to client: "Your website is live! Here's the link: [LINK]. 🎉"

**Handover**
1. Prepare handover document using the template above.
2. Send via WhatsApp (PDF) and email.
3. Schedule 30-minute walkthrough call.
4. On the call: show client their lead tracker, show them how to find the GBP, walk them through how a form submission arrives in their inbox.

**Internal Wrap-Up**
1. Update Notion project to: "Delivered."
2. Request remaining 50% payment.
3. Log any product observations (what was asked for that wasn't in scope? What did client struggle to understand? What would make this faster next time?).
4. Set reminder to follow up with client on Day 14 and Day 30.

---

### POST-DELIVERY FOLLOW-UP PROTOCOL

**Day 14 Check-In Message:**
> "Hi [Name], it's been two weeks since your website went live! How's it going — are you getting any inquiries through the site or WhatsApp? Would love to hear how things are going and if there's anything we can help with."

**Day 30 Check-In Message:**
> "Hi [Name], one month in! We'd love to understand how it's been working for you. Also — if you've been happy with the work, we'd really appreciate a short Google review or a quote we can use. And we wanted to share a couple of ideas for what could help you get even more from the system we've set up."

**Use Day 30 as the upsell moment.** Based on their response, pitch the natural next offering:
- If they're getting inquiries but losing them: pitch Offering 2 (Lead Capture + Chatbot)
- If they want more volume: pitch Offering 3 (Growth Support retainer)

---

## SOP Version Notes

This is Version 1.0. After every 3 deliveries, the team should review this SOP and ask:
1. What took longer than expected?
2. What did clients ask for that we didn't anticipate?
3. What can be templatized further?
4. What tools worked and what should be replaced?

Update the SOP accordingly. The goal is for Offering 1 to be deliverable in under 10 working days consistently and with minimal back-and-forth.
